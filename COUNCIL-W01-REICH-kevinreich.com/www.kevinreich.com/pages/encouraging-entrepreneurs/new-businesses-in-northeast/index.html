New businesses in Northeast

Be The Match Foundation, 3001 Broadway Street NE

  • The City of Minneapolis provided revenue bond funding to relocate and provide infrastructure improvements for this National Marrow Donor Program
  • The company employs 700 and will add 500 new jobs by 2019

The Broadway, 945 Broadway Street NE

  • The most recent undertaking in the industrial property reuse is the renovation of the Land O’ Nod Mattress factory. The plan calls for a mixed-use project with a combination of creative and office spaces.
  • The $4 million renovation will contain many features that honor the proximity of the Northeast Minneapolis Arts District, including a vertical glass gallery that will display artwork and an outdoor sculpture created by Zoran Mojsilov out of granite stones salvaged from the historic Metropolitan Building
  • One of the first tenants will be Sevnthsin, a digital creative agency. The Broadway will also welcome an exciting new addition to the Northeast Brew District – craft brewery 612Brew.

Burger King Site

  • Hillcrest Development has begun work on repurposing the long-neglected Burger King site at Central and 18th Avenue NE. A child Care facility will be opening in spring 2013.

Crescent Moon, 2339 Central Ave NE (and 1517 Como Ave SE)

  • The City helped secure a commercial corridor loan for building improvements, which helped increase the restaurant’s production capacity and made improvements in the dining area for the maker’s of our local award-winning football pizza.

Frost Building

  • For nearly 100 years the Frost Building, in the Northeast Park neighborhood, was home to a paint and linseed oil manufacturing company. This heavy industrial use produced noxious odors and left an immense amount of pollution in the buildings and grounds, including over 100 abandoned tanks of paint, oil and other waste.
  • Today, the Frost Building has been transformed by Hillcrest Development into two floors of warm, light-filled, and functional office space that houses creative businesses, like Modern Survey, Maren Kloppman Ceramics and Patrick Fox Photography

Mother Earth Gardens, coming soon to the corner of Lowry Avenue and Stinson Boulevard

  • The City helped work through regulations to better accommodate the expansion of this well established Longfellow neighborhood nursery, welcoming them to NE

Northgate Brewery, 3134 California Street NE

  • Another small brewery opened in Ward One in January 2013. Perched near the edge of the City’s northern border, Northgate joins 612Brew and Indeed in the new NE Brew District.

OATI: Open Access Technology International, 3660 Technology Drive NE

  • City of Minneapolis provided bonding to underwrite the relocation of this software service provider to former Honeywell building in the Mid-City Industrial neighborhood
  • Infrastructure improvements along 37th Ave and Technology Drive will help the company’s expansion grow from 300 employees to 600. Cable networking installed can be utilized by other area businesses and could attract new development

OceanTech, 1313 Winter Street NE in the Mid-City Industrial neighborhood

  • Relocating to NE in 2010, OceanTech specializes in electronics recycling, asset management services and secure data destruction. Specifically, they purchase a range of electronics, from computers and laptops to televisions and telephones, then refurbish or recycle them, ensuring they don’t become part of the growing electronics waste stream.

Recovery Bike Shop, 2525 Central Avenue NE, next to Eastside Food Co-op,

  • Opening in January of 2011, their focus is on providing affordable transportation by recycling old bicycles into hybrids, described as a cross between a mountain bike and a road bike.

Solar Arts Building, 711 15th Ave NE

  • One of the trademarks of Eastside Minneapolis is the creative renovation and reuse of our many industrial properties. Topped with 109 solar panels and filled with artist studios, a gallery and a microbrewery, today’s newly renovated Solar Arts Building is a far cry from its early 20th century roots as a site for the manufacture of tires, rubber and transformers.
  • Made possible by last year’s change in City ordinance and a stimulus loan for business start-up, Indeed Brewery features both a brewing operation and a taproom in the heart of what’s being called Northeast Minneapolis’ “Brew District”
  • The renovation also focused on enhancing the building’s creative space, including studios for artists and creative professionals and a 3rd floor gallery that hosted the Northeast Minneapolis Arts Association (NEMAA) Fall Fine Arts Show.
