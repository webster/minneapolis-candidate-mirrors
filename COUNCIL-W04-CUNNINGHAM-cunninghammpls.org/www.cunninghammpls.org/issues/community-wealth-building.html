Community Wealth Building

The Northside has been left out of fully participating in Minneapolis’ thriving economy. According to the Census, the annual average household income is $12,194 less than the rest of the city. Phillipe already works hard to address this injustice in City Hall. As your City Council representative, Phillipe will continue to fight to bring job training programs to the Northside. He will also fight for a Office of Small Business at City Hall to provide entrepreneurs from our community with resources to succeed and create more small business and therefore jobs in North Minneapolis.

In order for us to even begin addressing the economic inequities that plague our community, people first need to earn a livable wage that can help stabilize families and get them on the path toward economic prosperity. Phillipe supports a $15 minimum wage throughout the region in order to make sure Northsiders are receiving a livable wage regardless of whether or not they work within Minneapolis city limits.

Check back soon for videos that have more detailed information on Phillipe’s work with economic development and about a $15 minimum wage.
