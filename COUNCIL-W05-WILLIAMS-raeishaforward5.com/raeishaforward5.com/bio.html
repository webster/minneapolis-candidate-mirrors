Get to know Ra!

Biography

I grew up in a loving environment on 31st and Clinton in South Minneapolis.  My very existence as a woman was shaped by the protective and loving arms of my Grandmother Adelia Nevils, my Mother Rosemary Nevils-Williams, and many other community mothers and fathers.
I am Raeisha Williams, a friend, sister, auntie, daughter, community advocate, gardener, leader, and change maker. Many of you know me as the latter. What you may not know is that I am a former Ward 2 Coordinator for the Mayor’s Office of Washington, DC., that I graduated from Howard University with a B.A. in Mass Communications, that I  worked in television for the first half of my professional career, or that I have a strong background in nonprofits.
After completing my degree at Howard University, I had successful careers as a communications specialist with television networks including BET, TV One, and CNN. I worked as a publicist, marketing consultant, and community coordinator for nonprofits such as The ARC, DC Promise Neighborhood, and Metropolitan Regional Arts Council.

I worked as a community advocate for small social justice groups and recently completed a full term as the Communications Director for the NAACP Minneapolis Branch. I am the owner of a PR and marketing consulting firm with clients ranging from professional athletes like Ray Allen to powerhouse music producer Drumma Boy. I also created Twin Cities public announcement campaigns for NorthPoint Health and Wellness and Clearway.
I learned from my education and diverse career experiences that public service is my passion. Serving my community is my destiny. My career in politics started while attending North High School. As one of the inaugural members of Mayor Sharon Sayles Belton’s "Mayor Youth Council" I learned first hand what diplomacy and political engagement entailed.

My greatest calling aspiration is my decision to step forward and work with my community as the next City Council for Ward 5 in North Minneapolis. I take on this task with great pride in my community and a deep understanding of the responsibility ahead.

I look forward to working with you to create the "Best Ward 5" Minneapolis has ever seen.

In Solidarity,

Raeisha Williams
